summary: "Faustino Oro, a 12-year-old prodigy from Buenos Aires in the South American nation of Argentina, has earned the GM (Grandmaster, the highest title FIDE awards) title at the age of 12 years, six months, and 26 days — making him the second-youngest Grandmaster in chess history. He secured his third and final required norm at the Sardinia World Chess Festival in Italy, clinching it with a round to spare after defeating Poland's Bartlomiej Niedbala and then being paired against high-rated GM Ian Nepomniachtchi in the final round, which mathematically guaranteed the norm regardless of the result. Nicknamed \"The Messi of Chess,\" Oro trails only American GM Abhimanyu Mishra, who set the record in 2021 at 12 years, four months, and 25 days. FIDE's formal title confirmation awaits routine processing."
