Paul Morphy

New Orleans · 1837–1884

A century before Fischer, an American from New Orleans was the strongest player alive. Largely self-taught, Morphy crushed Europe’s best on a single 1858 tour. He retired at twenty-two, never playing seriously again — earning the title “the Pride and Sorrow of Chess.”

BOX 5 What Is a Gambit? 128 W

A gambit is a chess opening where a player deliberately sacrifices material — usually a pawn — for faster development, better piece activity, or an attack. The word comes from the Italian gambetto, meaning “a leg trip.” It first appeared in 1561 in a chess book by Ruy López de Segura. In everyday English a “gambit” describes any opening move in a negotiation where you give something small to gain something larger. Famous chess gambits include the King’s Gambit, the Queen’s Gambit, and the Evans Gambit.

The Long Road to the 64 Squares

History

Long before Morphy and Fischer, the game was born in India and carried west by Persian courts and Arab geographers. A history told from its source, not from Europe.

PERM A India · Chaturanga

A Game Named for an Army

The oldest known ancestor of chess is chaturanga, played in northern India by the sixth and seventh centuries during the Gupta era. Its name is Sanskrit for “having four limbs” — the four divisions of a classical Indian army: infantry, cavalry, elephantry, and chariotry. Those four arms survive on today’s board as the pawn, the knight, the bishop, and the rook. Most historians regard chaturanga as the common root of chess, Chinese xiangqi, Japanese shogi, and Thai makruk.

PERM B Persia & Arabia · Shatranj

The Geographer Who Wrote It Down

From India the game travelled to Sassanid Persia, where it became chatrang and entered the education of the nobility; after the Arab conquest the name softened into shatranj. Among those who recorded its story was al-Mas’udi (c. 896–956), the Baghdad-born historian called “the Herodotus of the Arabs.” It was through this Arabic and Persian world that chess crossed into Spain — not a European one.
