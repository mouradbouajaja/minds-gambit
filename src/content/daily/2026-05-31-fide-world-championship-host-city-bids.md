summary: "Today, May 31, marks the deadline for cities to submit bids to FIDE (the International Chess Federation) to host the 2026 World Chess Championship Match, with proposals due by 23:59 Lausanne time. The match, provisionally scheduled for November 23 through December 17, will pit defending champion Gukesh Dommaraju against challenger Javokhir Sindarov — the Uzbekistan (a Central Asian nation bordering Kazakhstan and Afghanistan) Grandmaster who won the 2026 Candidates Tournament — in what FIDE calls the youngest-ever World Championship match, as both players will be 20 years old when play begins. The $2.5 million prize fund matches that of the previous championship, and FIDE noted that India and Uzbekistan are the most logical host candidates given the players' home nations."
